String Family

The string family is the largest family of instruments and they come in 4 sizes the violin, viola, cello, and the double bass or sometimes called the contrabass(smallest to largest). Other strings family includes the guitar and the harp. The string family is an instrument wherein the sounds produced are from the strings. String instruments are chordophones which means that the sound that it produces are because of the vibrations of the strings.

Khorde=String & Phonos= Sound/Voice

The string instrument comprises of the neck, tuning pegs, and body. The neck is where the strings, fingerboard for violin, and fretboard for guitars comprises. Depending on the instrument the number of strings vary. For example a guitar has six strings while a violin has four. The strings on the neck also vary in weight to produce different pitches. Strings are either made up of nylon, steel or gut. The peg is where the strings are attached to, here they can be adjusted either to be tightened or loosened. Adjusting the pegs will change the pitch of the strings. Finally the body, comprised of different types of wood, and the main job of the body is to convert the vibrations of the string to sound.

A brief history of the stringed instruments is that they were first discovered in archeological sites of Ancient Mesopotamia. The instruments found here are lyres that were either plucked or played with a bow. This would then become the modern day violin and harp that we see. Fast forward to the medieval era and the development of the string family varied across Europe. Middle Eastern Rebec showed flashes of advancement in the areas of the body and the string.This is also the time wherein early versions of the violin and fiddle developed in the instruments such as glitten a ancestor of the guitar and some basic lutes.
