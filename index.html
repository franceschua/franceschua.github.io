my childhood years


I don't really remember much from my childhood years besides the fact that I was constantly in the honor roll. My parents were strict with my academics so I spent more time studying than playing with other children my age. There were several times when I would throw tantrums because I wanted to play, and not do my homeworks. I'd be met with a clothes hanger at home or a belt, but y'know, it be like that sometimes.
My mom has a large circle of friends, so, every few months, I find myself becoming a flower girl for weddings of people I didn't even know. It was kinda a bummer but with every wedding, there was food involved, so... I guess it was okay.
I really had no issues back then aside from the much too strict parenting I experienced, which became a major reason why I'm the way I am now.

my adolescent years


This was the time when issues caused by the childhood I had finally surfaced. I had the freedom that I never had when I was younger. As expected, I went all out. I was going out with my friends every week, galang gala my parents would say. My academics were slipping, and there was a time I was kicked off the volleyball time because of them, which was perfectly fine for me, smh. I didn't want to study, I didn't want to attend extra-curricular activites, I just wanted to have a good time.
Come grade eight, I was doing alright. During first and second term, I was receiving high marks and grades. In third term, however, I think I flipped a switch. Suddenly I wasn't doing good and I had massive depression. But yeah, it be like that sometimes. I failed chemistry, physics, and math that year (but my grades during the first half of the school year were high enought for me to pass, thank god).
Grade nine, I was gaining weight, probably from all the stress from the previous years. I grew self-conscious but I shifted my attention to academics instead of my appearance. I was constantly on top of my class.
In grade 10, I really don't know what happened. I sort of just went with the flow of things, so, whatever happened happened. Still, I kept my grades up, but they began to slip by the last term.

my body


Oh boy here we goooo. When I was still in the volleyball team, my team mates would often point out that I needed to drop some weight. I was by no means fat, I was actually quite fit then, but the sport required us to be in the best shape. My metabolism was w a c k then, I needed to eat a lot to be able to do the activities in training, otherwise I'd collapse.
In grade eight, I was the fittest in my batch. I had a slightly muscular build from all the training and work outs but I still had my baby fat. Post-depression me gained a lot of weight, and it didn't help that I quit the volleyball team as well, so there was no way for me to balance out what I ate with exercise.
In grade nine, I was what people considered fat. I had to buy a whole new set of uniforms because my oldest ones no longer fit me. I was conscious about my body then, but I didn't allow negative thoughts to linger for long.
Come grade 10, my parents started telling me to lose weight, to go to the gym or start eating less. I obviously ignored them, but their complaints were frequent to get under my skin.
In grade 11, I was too self-consious about my body to allow myself to attend prom. I told my classmates that my mom didn't allow me to go so they wouldn't know the real reason, still, I went to Makati Shang during prom day if only to spend time with them. I wore a cute casual dress and thought that I looked good. It was the first step of me coming to love my body.
Now that I'm in grade 12, there are still thoughts in my head that are like "lose weight", "you're fat", "too ugly", etc. I still manage to take selfies during the times I felt good about myself or whenever I felt cute or pretty. Yay for self-love.

my core values


I value a lot of things after my experiences in junior high school. I've learned the art of loving myself (hidden by my tendency of being self-deprecating) after a tough time of self inflicted scars and prayer. I've learned to let go my past mistakes to make way for more oppurtunies to do better, that I have to keep looking forward, to see the best in whatever situation even if it's nearly impossible.
I also learned that it's okay to set impossible goals (like me wanting to fling myself to the sun kasi ayoko na mag-aral pero joke lang mga kalahati) to motivate myself. It's never good to ignore your emotions, as I've learned the hard way, as they build up until you can no longer contain them, and they explode, damaging yourself and those around you.
Also, if you have to do something, be extra as hell, it adds a lot of fun.
I'm a person that has no initiative to approach others, which makes me a little bit socially awkward, but the willingness to try and get along with others keeps me going.

my dreams


During the course of my junior high school career, I wanted to be a lot of things. I wanted to be a doctor because I found it fun doing pretend surgery during that one time we went to dissect a pig's heart. However, that dream was wrecked to pieces after I found out that I had a case of hand tremors.
We had a creative writing course during grade seven, and I thoroughly enjoyed it, it was a fun way to make use of my imagination and creativity. However, my mom said that there's no money there, so goodbye dreams yet again.
Come eighth grade, we had journalism to replace creative writing. I really did consider going for Mass Communications for college after that, but decided against it because of my shy nature that time.
And then I wanted to become an architect after an art class where we were tasked to create our dream homes. My art teacher told me that I should consider becoming an architect, as she could see the potential that I had. I entertained the idea for a while, until I decided that it wasn't for me.
Now, I'm dead set on becoming a biomedical engineer. However, after seeing that it's a combination of three types of engineering (and possibly more), plus a lot of calculus and physics, I just want to drop out and never bother studying again (ayoko na magaral omg).

my fears


Honestly, I'm scared of change. I've been stuck in one place forfar too long and it's a scary idea not knowing where the next few steps would lead me to. If I were to take a step forward, what if I fall down and never get back up? What if I choose the wrong path, and taking a detour isn't a choice? At the same time, I'm scared of not moving, because others are already chasing after their dreams and aspirations and have a clear direction, whereas I am lost and unable to move forward. Very yikes for me.
I'm also scared of going back to my habit of self-harming. I've been clean for a couple of months now because of positive encouragement and warm acceptance from my friends. I'm afraid that, after one very bad experience, I'll go back and then I'll have another hard time to stop and get help from others.
